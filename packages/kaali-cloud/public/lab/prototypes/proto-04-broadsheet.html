A living paper on AI infrastructure

The Anatomy of a Request.

Every intelligent product is, at bottom, a request: a question leaves your code, crosses a wire, meets a model, and returns changed. Starvox Labs builds the infrastructure that carries that request — a gateway to twenty-five minds, a workflow engine that remembers its own state, and an operating system for the company around it. We publish our latencies the way a journal publishes its methods, because infrastructure that cannot be measured cannot be trusted.1 This page is the paper; the product is the proof.

§1 Gateway Fol. 2

One Key, Twenty-Five Minds.

A request enters at one door. Behind it stand twenty-five production models — reasoning, coding, vision, speech — reachable through a single key and a single, stable schema. The Gateway routes each call by capability, price, and observed latency; it fails over mid-flight when a provider degrades; it returns a response your code already knows how to read.

There is no SDK to adopt and no vendor to marry. Point your existing client at gpt.starvoxlabs.io, keep your prompts, and let the router argue with the providers on your behalf. Routing overhead is measured in microseconds; across the fleet the p50 is 214 ms and the p99 is 312 ms, observed from bom1, Mumbai.1

§2 Workflow Engine Fol. 3

Graphs That Hold Their State.

Most agent frameworks hold their state the way a lecture holds attention: badly, and only while running. The Workflow Engine treats a graph as a durable record. Every node execution is checkpointed; every edge decision is replayable; a crashed worker resumes at the exact step it died holding.

Where LangGraph gives you a library, we give you a ledger.2 Human gates are first-class citizens: a workflow can stop, wait three days for a signature, and continue as if no time had passed. Approval is a node, not an email thread.

§3 Company OS Fol. 4

The Company as a Document.

A company is a stack of documents pretending to be a building. Company OS keeps the pretence honest: e-sign for the agreements, finance for the invoices and the ledger behind them, auth and SSO for who may touch what. One operating record of the business, machine-readable end to end.

It runs on the same Gateway and the same Workflow Engine we sell. The invoice being approved in Fig 2.1 is not a metaphor — it is a row, a signature, and an audit line, in that order.
