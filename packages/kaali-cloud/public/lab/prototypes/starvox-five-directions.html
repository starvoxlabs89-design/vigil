Five
Directions

Five genuinely different sites, not five skins of one idea. Each sketch below is live — move your cursor through them. Every option is built to win at Awwwards; they differ in what they bet on, what they cost, and how high the ceiling goes.

OPTION 01

The Live Constellation

The site runs on the product, visibly. A cinematic star-field where every star is a real request through the gateway — color is latency, brightness is traffic. Scroll reorganizes the sky into each product: streams, graph, orbit.

LIVE SKETCH · TELEMETRY SKY

Narrative

Six acts, one artifact. Ignition loader → living sky → Gateway streams → Workflow graph → Company OS orbit → a footer that publishes the site's own p99. The story is evidence, not metaphor.

Signature moment

Hovering a star and watching it resolve into a real model card — live p50, real cost. The juror realizes the sky isn't decoration. That's the screenshot that travels.

Stack & effort

Next.js + Three.js/TSL + GSAP + Lenis + one SSE telemetry endpoint. ~4 weeks. Medium risk — particle choreography is proven tech; the telemetry feed is the only novel plumbing.

Awwwards read

The unclaimed conceit — no site on Awwwards is driven by live production data. Channels monopo's one-shader discipline + Shopify Globe's realness. Ceiling: SOTD certain-contender, SOTM possible.

OPTION 02

The Voyage

Ride a request through the machine. The whole site is one continuous camera flight: you enter as a token and travel the pipeline — tokenizer, router, model, back out — at request speed. Products are stations, not pages. Active Theory energy, Bruno Simon soul.

LIVE SKETCH · PIPELINE FLIGHT

Narrative

Scroll = throttle. You fly through glowing gates (auth → route → infer → return), each gate a product chapter rendered in-world. Nav is a star map. The infrastructure is a place you visit.

Signature moment

The router gate: your token splits into 25 light-trails, one per model, and the camera follows the fastest. Nobody has rendered an inference pipeline as a world — the device is sitting there unclaimed.

Stack & effort

Three.js/WebGPU, custom shaders, camera-on-spline, heavy asset work. 8–12 weeks, and it needs real 3D art direction. High risk: this is agency-flagship territory.

Awwwards read

The SOTY swing. Experience sites score highest (Oryzo 7.86, Messenger won Developer SOTY as a literal game). But mobile parity is brutal and one mediocre chapter sinks the whole flight. Ceiling: SOTM–SOTY. Floor: expensive miss.

OPTION 03

The Millisecond

F1 for inference. Lando Norris made speed a design language for a driver; we make it one for milliseconds. Fluorescent light-trails, scroll-scrubbed races, condensed italic type at brutal scale — every animation runs at a real, measured duration.

LIVE SKETCH · REAL-TIME RACES

Narrative

The homepage is a timing tower. Requests race across the viewport in their true durations; sections are laps (Gateway, Workflow, Company OS); a leaderboard ranks the 25+ models by live p50. Speed is the brand, proof is the content.

Signature moment

"Race the gateway": press and hold — a light bar crosses the screen in exactly one real p50 (~214ms) while your reflex tries to release in time. Visitors replay it, then screenshot their score. Built-in virality.

Stack & effort

GSAP + Lenis + light WebGL trails — mostly 2D craft, no heavy 3D. ~3 weeks. Low-medium risk; the hard part is copy and restraint, not tech.

Awwwards read

Channels the reigning Site of the Year's kinetic language but applied to an unclaimed subject (latency). Fourmula AI proved no-WebGL SOTD is winnable on animation craft alone. Ceiling: SOTD strong, SOTM stretch.

OPTION 04

The Broadsheet

A beautifully typeset systems paper, alive. While every AI company ships dark-glow-bento, we ship ink on paper: visible grid, kinetic editorial typography, dense/calm pacing — the Obys playbook aimed at infrastructure. The contrarian pick.

LIVE SKETCH · KINETIC TYPE ON GRID

Narrative

Reads like the definitive paper on AI infrastructure — abstract, figures, appendix — except every figure is live and every headline moves. Products are chapters with real diagrams, real numbers, footnoted claims. Authority as aesthetic.

Signature moment

Headlines typeset themselves as you scroll — letters sliding into the grid like a press laying type — and one "figure" is a live gateway chart rendered in print style. Screenshots look like no other AI site on earth.

Stack & effort

GSAP + custom smooth scroll + SVG/canvas figures. No heavy WebGL. 2–3 weeks — the cheapest and fastest option. Risk is craft-dependence: with average typography it's just a document.

Awwwards read

Obys proved editorial wins for deep tech (Glyphic, biotech). Design is 40% of the score and juries are visibly template-fatigued. Ceiling: SOTD on pure design merit. Weakness: lower "wow" for casual visitors.

OPTION 05

The Vox

The voice of the machine. Starvox = star + voice — so the brand is a single living waveform: an iridescent voiceprint that speaks. It reacts to your cursor, pulses with real traffic, and "pronounces" each section as you scroll. One perfect shader as the entire identity.

LIVE SKETCH · THE WAVEFORM — TOUCH IT

Narrative

The waveform is a character. It hums on the homepage, splits into 25 harmonics for the Gateway, knots into rhythm for Workflows, settles into a steady pulse for Company OS. Optional sound design makes it literal. One shader, whole brand.

Signature moment

Speak-to-the-site: click the mic and the waveform mirrors your voice, then answers with the gateway's — your words routed through a real model, visualized live. AI you can see listening.

Stack & effort

One custom fragment shader (OGL or Three.js) + GSAP + WebAudio. 2–3 weeks. Low risk, high polish-sensitivity — monopo's exact economics: one shader ≈ 90% of the feeling at 5% of the cost.

Awwwards read

Channels monopo london (SOTD + Dev Award on essentially one mouse-reactive shader). The mic moment gives it a creativity spike juries remember. Ceiling: SOTD. Sleeper pick — punches far above its build cost.

My call · primary

01 — The Live Constellation. It's the only direction with an unclaimed conceit ("real data as beauty"), it demonstrates the product while being beautiful, and it absorbs the best of the others: the Millisecond's motion physics, the Broadsheet's typography, the Vox's one-artifact discipline are already inside it. Best ratio of ceiling to risk, and the story writes its own press.

If we swing bigger · or leaner

Swing: 02 — The Voyage, if you'll spend 3 months and want a Site-of-the-Year attempt rather than SOTD. It has the highest ceiling of the five and nobody has claimed the device. Lean: 05 — The Vox, if we want award-tier in 3 weeks — monopo economics, plus the mic moment as the creativity spike. The honest anti-pick: 04 is gorgeous but its "wow" depends entirely on typographic execution the jury may under-reward on a product site.
