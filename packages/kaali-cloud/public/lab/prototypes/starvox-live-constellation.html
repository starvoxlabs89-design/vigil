01

The idea

Every AI company shows you a mockup. We show you the heartbeat.

One conceit, executed totally. The homepage is a single WebGL star-field where each particle is born from a real, anonymized request flowing through gpt.starvoxlabs.io — its color is the response latency, its brightness the model's traffic. The counter is real. The motion is real. When India wakes up, the sky gets busier.

This is the whitespace nobody has claimed. Active Theory built a world, Bruno Simon built a game, monopo built a shader — all beautiful, all fiction. The entire AI category, meanwhile, has collapsed into the same dark-glow-bento template. No one has shipped a marketing site whose beauty is generated by live production data.

The constellation is the only 3D object on the site — and it never leaves. As you scroll, it reorganizes into each product: scattered signal becomes the Gateway's routed streams, streams become the Workflow Engine's executing graph, the graph settles into Company OS's orbit. One artifact, four states, zero gimmicks around it.

Everything else is restraint: print-grade editorial typography, a two-color chrome, native scroll. The spectacle is concentrated in one place and it happens to be true. That's the sentence a juror writes down: "the site runs on the thing it sells."

03

The physics — rules of the universe

Design language

The system has three laws. Everything on the site — every color, every curve, every duration — derives from them. Nothing is decorative; if a value can come from the gateway, it does.

Law 1 · Color is temperature

Stars are colored by response latency, exactly like real stellar classification: hot and fast burns blue-white, slow cools through amber to ember red. The palette isn't a mood board — it's a legend.

Law 2 · Motion is measurement

Animation durations and easing curves are sampled from the gateway's real latency distribution. A hover card opens in one p50. A formation morph takes one p99. The brand literally moves at the speed of the product.

Law 3 · Chrome is silent, the sky is loud

Around the constellation: near-void black, warm starlight text, hairline rules, visible grid. Two colors of chrome, maximum — so the sky can burn as bright as it wants. All the drama, concentrated in the one thing that's true.

The instrument panel

Interaction inventory

  • CURSOR · GRAVITY WELLThe pointer bends nearby starlight — subtle attraction within ~140px, never a novelty cursor. The mouse is an instrument, not a decoration.
  • HOVER · RESOLVEAny star resolves into its model card with live p50 and pricing. On touch: tap. The catalog of 25+ models is explorable sky, not a pricing table (the table still exists, one click away — usability is 30% of the score).
  • SCROLL · FORMATIONNative scroll via Lenis, position mapped to formation morphs. Scroll-driven, never scroll-hijacked — the documented #1 Awwwards mark-down. You can flick to the footer at full speed and nothing fights you.
  • PRESENCE · AMBIENTA small honest counter: “14 engineers watching this sky.” For a product whose pitch is scale, visible concurrency is self-demonstrating (the Active Theory v6 move, minus avatars).
  • SOUND · OPTIONALOff by default. A low observatory hum plus one soft ping when a formation locks. A visible toggle, state remembered.
  • EASTER EGG · SUPERNOVATriple-click the brightest star: it goes supernova and reveals build credits. Every recent winner hides one; jurors hunt for them.
  • REDUCED MOTION · ENGRAVINGSprefers-reduced-motion swaps the living sky for still star-chart engravings of each formation, and the ticker becomes a static log. Fully designed, not a fallback.
06

Negative space — what this site refuses to do

The refusals

  • No glow-bento template. Purple gradients, glassy cards, marquee logo walls — the COSMOQ look reads “another AI wrapper” to a jury in 3 seconds.
  • No scroll-jacking. Scroll distance is sacred. Animations read scroll position; they never own it.
  • No fake numbers. If a counter can't be real, it doesn't ship. Simulated fallback exists only for quiet hours and is architecturally identical.
  • No 5-second preloader. Budget: LCP under 1.5s, under 3MB total, 60fps sustained. Winners' bar, our floor.
  • No wall-to-wall 3D. One artifact, revisited. Full-world WebGL is for agencies selling WebGL.
  • No desktop-only spectacle. Mobile gets the same sky at capped DPR and particle count — parity, not apology. Responsive is a scored Developer-Award criterion.
